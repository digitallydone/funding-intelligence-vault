# Customer Onboarding Playbook
**BOSs | Business Opportunity Systems — Funding Intelligence Vault**

---

## Purpose

This playbook walks a new customer through getting maximum value from the Funding Intelligence Vault in their first week. It can be shared directly with customers or used as the basis for onboarding emails and in-app guidance.

---

## Day 0 — Access & Setup (15 minutes)

### Step 1: Copy the workspace to your Google Drive

1. Open the Google Sheets template link from your confirmation email or `customer-start.html`
2. Click **File** in the top menu
3. Click **Make a copy**
4. Name it something useful: `Funding Intelligence Vault — [Your Org Name] — [Year]`
5. Choose where to save it in your Drive
6. Click **OK** — you now own a full copy

> Your copy is completely independent. You can edit, delete, sort, and customize it freely without affecting the original.

### Step 2: Explore the columns

Take 5 minutes to scroll through the columns:

| Column | What it tells you |
|---|---|
| **Funder / Program** | The organization and specific grant program |
| **Country/Region & Province** | Geographic scope and eligibility |
| **Sector** | Primary focus area of the funding |
| **Eligible Applicants** | Who can apply (charity, nonprofit, Indigenous org, etc.) |
| **Funding Type** | Project, operating, capital, wage subsidy, etc. |
| **Typical Amount** | Funding range for a typical grant |
| **Deadline Type** | Rolling, annual, bi-annual, invitation-only |
| **Next Deadline** | Known upcoming deadline or cycle |
| **Match Required** | Whether a cost-share or matching contribution is needed |
| **Website** | Direct link to the funder's program page |
| **Fit Score** | High / Medium / Low — our pre-assessment of fit for a typical nonprofit |
| **Difficulty** | Low / Medium / High — complexity of the application process |
| **Notes** | Key context, eligibility nuances, and strategic notes |
| **Status** | Your tracking field — update as you research and apply |
| **Next Action** | What to do next with this opportunity |

## Day 1–2 — Filter for Your Context (30 minutes)

### Step 1: Filter by your province

Use the **Province/State** column filter to show only funders relevant to your geography. National funders (marked "National") are always relevant regardless of where you're based.

### Step 2: Filter by your sector

Use the **Sector** column to narrow to programs aligned with your mandate. Don't filter too tightly — many funders support cross-sector work.

### Step 3: Sort by Fit Score, then Difficulty

Sort the **Fit Score** column to put High-fit opportunities at the top. Then, within High-fit, sort by **Difficulty** (Low first) to identify your easiest entry points.

### Step 4: Build your shortlist

Add a new column called **Priority** and mark your top 10–15 funders as Priority 1, 2, or 3. This is your active pipeline.

---

## Day 3–5 — Research Your Priority Funders (2–3 hours)

For each Priority 1 funder:

1. Click the **Website** link to open their program page
2. Confirm your eligibility (organization type, geography, sector)
3. Note the next deadline in your calendar
4. Update the **Status** column: `Researching`, `Qualified`, `Cultivating`, or `Not Eligible`
5. Update the **Next Action** column with a specific task and date

> **Rule of thumb:** Don't start writing an application until you've confirmed eligibility on the funder's own website. Fit Score is our pre-assessment — always verify.

---

## Week 2 — Build Your Pipeline Rhythm

### Set up deadline reminders

For every qualified funder with a known deadline, set a calendar reminder:
- 8 weeks before deadline: begin application
- 4 weeks before: first draft complete
- 1 week before: final review and submission

### Assign ownership (for teams)

If you're working with a team, use the **Next Action** column to assign tasks. Add a column for **Owner** if helpful.

### Track your status

Keep the **Status** column updated. Recommended status values:
- `Research` — identified, not yet reviewed in detail
- `Qualified` — confirmed eligible and aligned
- `Cultivating` — building relationship before applying
- `Applied` — application submitted
- `Awarded` — funding confirmed
- `Declined` — not funded this cycle
- `Not Eligible` — confirmed ineligible, archive or revisit later
- `On Hold` — pausing for now, revisit next cycle
