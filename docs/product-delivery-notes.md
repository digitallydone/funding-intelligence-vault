# Product Delivery Notes
**BOS | Business Opportunity Systems — Funding Intelligence Vault**
**Version: 1.0 — Early Access**

---

## Product Overview

The Funding Intelligence Vault is a pre-populated funding research workspace delivered primarily as a Google Sheets copy-template. It contains 75 curated Canadian funding opportunities across 17 research columns, designed to give nonprofits, NGOs, social enterprises, and grant writers a ready-to-use funding intelligence system.

---

## What the Customer Receives

### Primary delivery
- **Google Sheets workspace** — a shared template link allowing the customer to make a personal copy in their Google Drive. The workspace contains all 75 funding opportunities with all 17 columns pre-filled.

### Secondary delivery
- **CSV export** — the full dataset in UTF-8 CSV format, importable into any tool (Notion, Airtable, Monday.com, custom CRM, etc.)

### Included bonus
- **Free guide** — *The Canadian Funding Landscape: Where to Start* — a five-chapter orientation guide, hosted at `guide-full.html`

### Optional add-on (white-glove)
- **Airtable base setup** — manual configuration of the customer's Airtable base using the spec in `airtable-base-spec.md`. Requested via email after purchase.

## Dataset Details

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Total records | 75 |
| Columns | 17 |
| Geography | Canada (national + provincial) |
| Sectors covered | Arts & Culture, Community Development, Environment, Health, Youth, Education, Indigenous Programming, Social Enterprise, Women, Disability, Sport, Capacity Building |
| Provinces/territories represented | National, ON, BC, AB, MB, QC, NS, Nunavik |
| File format | CSV (UTF-8), Google Sheets |
| Data currency | Current as of product launch — users should verify individual funder details on funder websites |

### Column definitions

1. **Funder** — Name of the funding organization (government department, foundation, corporation)
2. **Program** — Specific grant program name within that organization
3. **Country/Region** — Canada (all records) / regional notes where applicable
4. **Province/State** — Primary geographic scope (National or specific province)
5. **Sector** — Primary funding sector(s)
6. **Eligible Applicants** — Organization types that can apply
7. **Funding Type** — Project / Operating / Capital / Wage Subsidy / Partnership / Capacity
8. **Typical Amount** — Funding range for a typical successful application
9. **Deadline Type** — Rolling / Annual / Bi-annual / Invitation / LOI
10. **Next Deadline** — Known upcoming deadline (approximate; verify with funder)
11. **Match Required** — Whether matching funds or in-kind contribution is required
12. **Website** — Direct URL to the program page
13. **Fit Score** — High / Medium / Low — pre-assessed fit for a typical mid-size nonprofit
14. **Difficulty** — Low / Medium / High — application complexity and competitiveness
15. **Notes** — Strategic context, eligibility nuances, relationship requirements
16. **Status** — Customer-editable tracking field
17. **Next Action** — Customer-editable action item

---

## Known Limitations (v1.0)

- Dataset is focused exclusively on Canadian funding; international or US-based funders are not included in v1
- Fit Score and Difficulty are generalized assessments — individual organizations should verify based on their specific mandate and capacity
- Funding amounts, deadlines, and eligibility criteria change — customers should treat the dataset as a starting point and verify details on funder websites
- No automated deadline tracking or reminders (this is a manual workspace)
- No login/access control in v1 — `customer-start.html` is protected only by obscurity

## Data Maintenance

The dataset should be reviewed and updated at minimum:
- Quarterly (deadlines and amounts)
- Annually (full content review)
- As needed when customers report outdated information

Assign a team member to own dataset currency. Stale data is the primary product quality risk.
